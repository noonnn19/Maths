a small proof

You like math…
so I tried to make sense of
something using it.

There's just one rule —
you have to solve each one first.

equation no. 1

(x² + y² − 1)³ − x²y³ = 0

What shape does this equation plot?

Think about what I'd graph just for you…

Okay… this one was easy.

equation no. 2

d = √((x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²)

If x₁ = 0, y₁ = 0, x₂ = 3, y₂ = 4 —
what is d?

Pythagorean theorem — 3² + 4² = ?

They say distance is just a formula.

But this one feels a little unfair.

equation no. 3

limx→∞  1/x = ?

As x grows forever, where does 1/x end up?

The bigger x gets, the smaller 1/x becomes…

Some things go to zero over time.

This isn't one of them.

equation no. 4

y = mx + c

In this equation, what does c represent?

It's the part that never changes, no matter what x does.

Everything changes.

But some constants stay.

I tried graphs.

Limits.

Formulas.

None of them explain you.

So I'll just say it.
I love you. A lot.
